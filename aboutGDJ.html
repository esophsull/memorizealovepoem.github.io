About Georgia Douglas Johnson

Georgia Douglas Johnson was a playwright and poet. She was born in Atlanta, Georgia in 1880 and lived for eighty-five years. She was central to the anti-lynching movement and a member of the Writers League Against Lynching. She was the author of a weekly newspaper column, a church organist, and music educator.

Following the death of her husband, politician Henry Lincoln Johnson, Georgia established the S Street Salon out of their family home in Washington D.C. Johnson's weekly salons were attended by major figures of the Harlem Renaissance, including Zora Neale Hurston, Langston Hughes, Jessie Faucet, and many others-- attendees were known as the "Saturday Nighters."
