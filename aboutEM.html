About Edwin Morgan

Edwin Morgan was born in Glasgow in 1920. In 2004, he was crowned Makar, making him the first national poet of Scotland, a position which he held until the year of his death in 2010. He translated works from Hungarian, Russian, Latin and other languages into English, including his 1952 translation of Beowulf.

Morgan was beloved by poets around the world and by his fellow Glaswegians, who crowned him the first Glasgow Poet Laureate in 1999.
